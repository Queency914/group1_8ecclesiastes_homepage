Water cycle
The water cycle is the path that all water follows as it moves around Earth in different states.

Evaporation
This is the first step in the water cycle. It is when the sun heats up a water source which turns the water to water vapor, basically liquid to gas.

Condensation
As the water vapor rises higher and higher into the atmosphere, the cool air causes the water vapor back into liquid, forming clouds.

Precipitation
When a cloud becomes full of liquid water, it falls from the sky as rain or snow

Collection
Rain and snow then fill lakes and streams. Plants also absorb water, but not all of it, which allows the process to start all over again.

 
	 Importance of the water cycle:
    One of the most vital components of the environment is water, which sustains life for humans, animals, and plants alike. If the water were to stop flowing, disaster would follow, but the water cycle keeps that from happening.
